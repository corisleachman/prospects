( 02 )

The build, step by step

Five moves. The only mildly fiddly one is Google, so that gets its own walk-through below.

01

Open three free accounts

Sign up for GitHub (hosting) and Supabase (database), and have your Google account ready. All free, a minute each, no card needed.

02

Describe the page to an AI

Open your AI assistant and talk in outcomes, not tech: "Build me a page that lists my prospects and lets me email them, one at a time or in bulk." It asks a couple of questions, then writes the whole page and pulls your existing contacts in.

03

Hand over three keys

A "key" is just a temporary password you can delete afterwards. You create one on each service and paste it to the AI — it does the rest.

  • Supabase: create an access token → the AI builds your database and loads your contacts.
  • GitHub: create a repo and a token → the AI uploads the page.
  • Google: set up the sign-in and copy the Client ID → this is what lets the page send email (walk-through below).
04

Turn the page on

In your GitHub repository, flip on Pages (Settings → Pages → deploy from main / root). That's the free "publish" button. Give it a minute and your page is live on the web.

05

Connect Google and send

Open your live page, click Connect Google once and approve it. Pick a contact, choose a template, and hit send — it goes out from your real Gmail. That's the whole loop.

The one fiddly bit

The Google permission screen, click by click

All you're doing is telling Google "this page of mine is allowed to send email from my account." Go slowly and follow each click.

  1. Make a project. Go to console.cloud.google.com → project dropdown → New Project → name it "Prospects" → select it.
  2. Turn on the Gmail engine. APIs & Services → Library → search Gmail API → Enable.
  3. Name your app. OAuth consent screen (newer console: Google Auth Platform → Branding) → choose External → fill app name, support email, developer email.
  4. Say who can use it — you. Under Audience / Test users, add your own email, or click Publish app to skip the weekly re-login.
  5. Create the key. Credentials → Create credentials → OAuth client ID → type: Web application → name it.
  6. Allow your page. Authorised JavaScript origins: https://yourname.github.io — Authorised redirect URIs: https://yourname.github.io/your-page/ → Create.
  7. Copy the Client ID (it ends in apps.googleusercontent.com) and paste it to the AI. Done.

First time you connect, Google shows an "unverified app" warning — that's normal for a personal tool you just made. Click Advanced → continue, then approve.

( 03 )

Then make it yours

Because it's your page, every new feature is just another sentence to the AI. Here's what I added to mine.

Saveable templates

Write a message once, save it as a pill, reuse it in a click — with names filled in per person.

Follow-up nudges

Email someone and a chase-up is scheduled automatically, stacking up in a side pane so nothing slips.

Attachments

Send files with your emails, individually or across a whole batch.

Conversation view

Read the full back-and-forth with a contact right on the page — no hopping back to Gmail.

Three sign-ups. Describe it. Paste three keys. Hit send.

That's the whole trick. The "developer" is a conversation, and the page grows with you.
